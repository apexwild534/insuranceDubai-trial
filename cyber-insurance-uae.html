Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about this insurance type in Dubai and UAE.

What is cyber insurance in UAE?

Cyber insurance provides financial protection against losses from cyber attacks, data breaches and technology failures. It covers your own costs and liability to third parties affected by a cyber incident.

Is cyber insurance mandatory in UAE?

Cyber insurance is not yet universally mandated, but the UAE Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL) creates significant liability for data breaches, making cyber insurance increasingly essential for UAE businesses.

What does cyber insurance NOT cover?

Most cyber policies exclude pre-existing known vulnerabilities, state-sponsored attacks (war exclusion), infrastructure failures and losses resulting from failure to maintain basic security standards.

How is cyber insurance premium calculated?

Cyber insurance premiums are based on revenue, industry sector, number of records held, security posture (controls, MFA, backups) and claims history. UAE premiums typically range from AED 5,000 to AED 50,000+ for SMEs.
