How an ILOE Claim Works

Eligibility, payout and timing — what you actually get if you are made redundant.

1

Qualifying Job Loss

You must have been terminated due to redundancy, restructuring or company closure — not for resignation, disciplinary action, or contract end. You also need 12 consecutive months of premium contributions.

→
2

File Within 30 Days

Submit your claim via the ILOE portal, ILOE app, or call centre 600 599 555 within 30 days of termination. Required documents: cancelled work permit, last 6 payslips and Emirates ID.

→
3

Get 60% of Salary, 3 Months

You receive 60% of your average basic salary from the previous 6 months, paid for up to 3 consecutive months — capped at AED 10,000/month (Cat A) or AED 20,000/month (Cat B).

Frequently Asked Questions — ILOE 2026

The questions UAE residents ask most about the MOHRE Involuntary Loss of Employment scheme.

Is ILOE insurance the same as MOHRE insurance?

Yes. "MOHRE insurance" is the colloquial name UAE residents use for the ILOE scheme because it is administered by the Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation. The official product is the Involuntary Loss of Employment Insurance Scheme issued by Dubai Insurance Co. on behalf of MOHRE.

Can I claim ILOE if I resign?

No. ILOE pays only when termination is involuntary — typically redundancy, business closure, or restructuring. Voluntary resignation, contract expiry without renewal, and disciplinary dismissal are excluded.

How long until I get my first ILOE payout?

Once your claim is approved, payment is made within 14 working days into your bank account. Claims must be filed within 30 days of contract termination, otherwise they will be rejected.

What is the difference between ILOE and "job loss insurance UAE"?

ILOE is the UAE's official job-loss insurance — and it is mandatory. Some private insurers (Sukoon, GIG Gulf, Liva) also sell top-up job loss insurance UAE products that pay over and above the ILOE benefit cap. We can compare both for you.

Can I subscribe to ILOE without my employer?

Yes — ILOE premiums are paid directly by the employee, not the employer. You can subscribe independently through the ILOE portal, app, telecom outlets or via a licensed aggregator like InsuranceDubai.com.
