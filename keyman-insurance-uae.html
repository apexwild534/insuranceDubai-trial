What is Keyman Insurance in UAE?

Keyman insurance (also called key person insurance) compensates your business if a critical individual dies or becomes seriously ill.

In any UAE business, certain individuals drive a disproportionate share of revenue, client relationships, technical expertise or leadership. The sudden death or critical illness of such a person can be catastrophic — causing lost contracts, reduced revenue, recruitment costs and potential business failure.

Keyman insurance provides a tax-free lump sum to the business on the death or critical illness of the insured key person. This cash injection protects the business while it recovers, recruits a replacement or winds down obligations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about this insurance type in Dubai and UAE.

Who qualifies as a keyman in UAE?

A keyman is any individual whose loss would significantly impact the business. This typically includes founders, managing directors, lead salespeople, key technical staff and sole traders.

How is the sum insured calculated for keyman insurance?

The sum insured is typically 3–5 times the key person's annual salary or their estimated contribution to business profit. Lenders sometimes specify a minimum sum for loan-backed policies.

Is keyman insurance tax-deductible in UAE?

The UAE currently has no corporate income tax for most businesses, so this is less relevant. Proceeds from keyman policies are generally tax-free to the business in the UAE.

What is the difference between keyman and shareholder protection?

Keyman insurance protects the business against the loss of a valuable employee. Shareholder protection ensures surviving shareholders can buy out a deceased partner's share without personal financial strain.
