2025 Federal Health Insurance Mandate — What Changed

The biggest shake-up to UAE healthcare in 20 years. Most competitor pages still haven't updated for it.

From 1 January 2025, UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021 (with Cabinet Decision No. 11 of 2024) made health insurance mandatory for every private-sector employee and domestic worker — across Sharjah, Ajman, Ras Al Khaimah, Fujairah and Umm Al Quwain, joining Abu Dhabi (since 2006) and Dubai (since 2014).

  • ✓  Employer pays 100% — no employee contribution allowed
  • ✓  Cover must be active before or on day one; no grace period
  • ✓  No new visa or work-permit issuance/renewal without a linked policy (live MOHRE-ICP gateway)
  • ✓  AED 320/year federal Basic Plan for the Northern Emirates — see plan details
  • ✓  Direct billing now live across 380+ network providers (as of Q1 2026)
  • ⚠️  Fines: AED 500–2,500 per uncovered worker per month; up to AED 500,000 for repeat company offences

Your Health Insurance Obligations by Residency Type

Employees, dependents, self-sponsored residents, freelancers, and family sponsors all have different obligations.

👔 Employees (Private Sector)

Your employer is legally required to provide health insurance before or on day one of employment. You cannot be asked to contribute. Cover must meet the minimum EBP standards in Dubai, or the AED 320 MOHRE Basic Plan in the Northern Emirates. If your employer fails to cover you, they face fines of AED 500–2,500 per month per uncovered worker.

👨‍👩‍👧 Family Sponsors

If you sponsor a spouse, children or parents on a family visa, you are responsible for their health insurance — it is not provided by your employer. Cover must be in place to issue or renew UAE residence visas. Family plans covering a spouse and up to three children typically run AED 5,000–15,000 per year depending on age and network.

🆔 Self-Sponsored Residents & Freelancers

On a freelance permit, investor visa, retirement visa or self-sponsored residence visa? You must arrange and pay for your own health insurance. Plans start from around AED 1,800/year for basic individual cover. Without an active policy, you cannot renew your UAE residence visa through the ICA gateway.

🏠 Domestic Workers

If you employ a housemaid, driver, nanny or any live-in domestic worker, you are required to provide them with health insurance under the UAE federal mandate. The AED 320 MOHRE Basic Plan covers this obligation in the Northern Emirates. In Dubai, the DHA Essential Benefits Plan is the minimum requirement.

What Does Health Insurance Cover in UAE?

Understanding your UAE health insurance coverage — and the most Googled question in Dubai: what does the EBP actually include?

✅ What EBP Does Cover

  • ✓  Emergency medical treatment at DHA-approved facilities
  • ✓  In-patient hospitalisation and surgery
  • ✓  Out-patient GP and specialist consultations
  • ✓  Diagnostic tests, lab work and imaging
  • ✓  Prescription medications at network pharmacies
  • ✓  Maternity care from conception through post-natal
  • ✓  Physiotherapy (limited sessions)
  • ✓  Pre-existing conditions (after 6-month waiting period)

❌ What EBP Does NOT Cover

  • ✗  Dental treatment (except emergency extractions)
  • ✗  Optical / eyeglasses and contact lenses
  • ✗  Mental health and psychiatric treatment
  • ✗  Cosmetic or elective procedures
  • ✗  International or out-of-country cover
  • ✗  Private hospital rooms (only shared wards)
  • ✗  High-cost cancer treatments and biologics
  • ✗  Medical evacuation and repatriation

What Affects Your Health Insurance Premium in UAE?

Understanding these factors helps pre-qualify you for the right plan — and increases the accuracy of your quote.

🎂

Age

Premiums increase significantly at age 40, 50 and 60. Younger applicants qualify for lower base rates across all tiers.

🏥

Network Tier

Access to private hospitals (e.g. American Hospital, Mediclinic City) costs 30–60% more than plans limited to network clinics only.

🤰

Maternity Cover

Adding maternity cover to an individual plan can add AED 2,000–8,000 to annual premiums. Group plans usually include it.

📋

Pre-Existing Conditions

Diabetes, hypertension, cardiac conditions and obesity can result in loadings of 15–40% or exclusions for the first 6–12 months.

🌍

International Cover

Worldwide coverage (especially including the USA) can triple your premium versus UAE-only plans. Consider whether you genuinely need it.

👨‍👩‍👧

Number of Dependents

Each additional family member adds to premium. Children are typically cheaper to add; a second spouse or parent sponsor increases cost substantially.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about health insurance in UAE and Dubai.

Is health insurance mandatory in UAE?

Yes, across all seven emirates as of 1 January 2025. All private-sector employers must provide health insurance for employees. Residents who are self-employed, freelancers, or dependents must arrange their own cover to issue or renew UAE residence visas.

What is the DHA Essential Benefits Plan?

The EBP is the minimum DHA-mandated health insurance for lower-income workers in Dubai. It covers emergency care, basic out-patient, maternity and diagnostic services at specific networks. It does not include dental, optical or mental health cover.

Can I use my Dubai health insurance in Abu Dhabi?

Most comprehensive plans cover treatment across the UAE. Basic or EBP plans may be restricted to Dubai only. Always check your policy's geographical coverage area before travelling within the UAE for treatment.

How do I renew health insurance in Dubai?

Your employer typically handles renewal for employee plans. For individual or family plans, InsuranceDubai.com sends renewal reminders 30 days before expiry and compares market options at no extra cost.

'UAE insurance' vs 'health insurance Dubai' — what's the difference?

'UAE insurance' is a broader term covering all seven emirates under the 2025 federal mandate. 'Health insurance Dubai' specifically refers to DHA-regulated plans. Both are mandatory, but the minimum plan, regulator and network differ by emirate. Search volume for UAE-wide terms is now 40% higher than Dubai-specific terms.
