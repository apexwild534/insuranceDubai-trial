2025 Federal Health Insurance Mandate — What Changed

The biggest shake-up to UAE healthcare in 20 years. Most competitor pages still haven't updated for it.

From 1 January 2025, UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021 (with Cabinet Decision No. 11 of 2024) made health insurance mandatory for every private-sector employee and domestic worker — across Sharjah, Ajman, Ras Al Khaimah, Fujairah and Umm Al Quwain, joining Abu Dhabi (since 2006) and Dubai (since 2014).

  • ✓  Employer pays 100% — no employee contribution allowed
  • ✓  Cover must be active before or on day one; no grace period
  • ✓  No new visa or work-permit issuance/renewal without a linked policy (live MOHRE-ICP gateway)
  • ✓  AED 320/year federal Basic Plan for the Northern Emirates — see plan details
  • ✓  Direct billing now live across 380+ network providers (as of Q1 2026)
  • ⚠️  Fines: AED 500–2,500 per uncovered worker per month; up to AED 500,000 for repeat company offences

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about this insurance type in Dubai and UAE.

Is health insurance mandatory in Dubai?

Yes. All Dubai employers must provide health insurance for employees. Residents who are self-employed or dependents must arrange their own cover to renew UAE residence visas.

What is the DHA Essential Benefits Plan?

The EBP is the minimum DHA-mandated health insurance for lower-income workers in Dubai. It covers emergency care, basic out-patient, maternity and diagnostic services at specific networks.

Can I use my Dubai health insurance in Abu Dhabi?

Most comprehensive plans cover treatment across the UAE. Basic plans may be restricted to Dubai only. Always check your policy's geographical coverage area.

How do I renew health insurance in Dubai?

Your employer typically handles renewal for employee plans. For individual or family plans, InsuranceDubai.com sends renewal reminders 30 days before expiry and compares market options.
