10 Ways to Save Money on Motor Insurance in UAE

Expert, tested strategies to reduce your car insurance premium in Dubai and the UAE — without compromising the coverage you need.

Motor insurance is one of the largest recurring insurance expenses for most UAE residents. With car insurance costs rising in recent years, knowing how to legally reduce your premium without compromising your protection is more important than ever. Here are 10 expert strategies to reduce your Dubai motor insurance costs.

1

Compare Multiple Insurers Via Our Aggregator

The single biggest saving comes from comparing multiple insurers simultaneously. InsuranceDubai.com compares 27+ UAE motor insurers in real time, and our clients save an average of 23% versus renewing with their current insurer.

2

Protect Your No-Claims Discount (NCD)

If you have built up a no-claims discount, protect it with an NCD protector rider. This allows you to make one claim per year without losing your discount — preserving substantial savings in future years.

3

Choose a Higher Voluntary Excess

Increasing your voluntary excess (the amount you pay before the insurer covers a claim) from AED 500 to AED 2,000 can reduce your premium by 10–20%. Only do this if you have savings to cover the excess.

4

Opt for Non-Agency Repair for Older Vehicles

Agency repair (manufacturer dealership) adds 20–40% to your premium. For cars over 3 years old, non-agency repair provides the same practical protection at significantly lower cost.

5

Pay Annually Rather Than Monthly

Many UAE insurers charge a loading of 8–15% for monthly payment plans. If cash flow allows, paying your premium annually delivers an immediate saving.

6

Add Security Features to Your Vehicle

Immobilisers, GPS trackers and dash cameras are valued by UAE insurers as risk-reduction devices. Installing approved security features can result in discounts of 5–15%.

7

Bundle with Other Insurance Products

Many UAE insurers offer multi-policy discounts of 10–20% when you combine motor insurance with home, life or health insurance. InsuranceDubai.com can find you the best bundled deals.

8

Declare a Lower Annual Mileage

If you work from home or use your car infrequently, declaring a lower annual mileage can reduce your premium. Be accurate — mileage misrepresentation can invalidate a claim.

9

Remove Unnecessary Add-Ons

Review your policy for add-ons you don't need — roadside assistance (if covered by your car manufacturer), passenger personal accident, or GCC cover if you never leave the UAE. Every removal saves money.

10

Renew Early — Don't Let Your Policy Lapse

Renewing your motor insurance 2–4 weeks before expiry gives you time to compare the market without pressure. Letting your policy lapse even for one day means you lose your NCD, costing significantly more at the next renewal.
