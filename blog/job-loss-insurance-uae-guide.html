Two products, one outcome: salary protection

"Job loss insurance" in the UAE is not a single product. It is a stack of two:

  • The ILOE scheme — federally mandated since January 2023 under Decree-Law No. 13 of 2022. Administered by Dubai Insurance Co. on behalf of MOHRE. Pays 60% of basic salary, capped at AED 10,000 (Cat A) or AED 20,000 (Cat B) per month, for 3 months per claim and 12 months lifetime.
  • Private job-loss insurance UAE top-ups — voluntary cover sold by Sukoon, GIG Gulf, Liva, NAS, Nextcare and others. Pays the gap above ILOE, often up to 80% of total salary for 6, 9 or 12 months.

Together they form a robust safety net. Alone, ILOE is enough to keep a junior professional afloat for one quarter; a private top-up is what stops a senior expat from missing a mortgage payment if a layoff drags on.

The mandatory ILOE scheme — recap

If you are a private-sector or federal-government employee in the UAE, you must already be paying ILOE premiums. As of 29 April 2026 the structure is:

To claim, you must have paid premiums for at least 12 consecutive months, file within 30 days of involuntary termination, and not have left the country. Resignation, contract end and disciplinary dismissal are all excluded. Importantly, the AED 10,000/AED 20,000 caps are based on basic salary only — for a senior expat earning, say, AED 60,000 a month total package, ILOE replaces less than 25% of real income.

What private job loss insurance UAE actually adds

Private "job loss insurance UAE" — sometimes sold as income protection, salary continuation or redundancy rider — fills three specific gaps:

  • Higher monthly cap. Up to AED 75,000 / month for senior policyholders.
  • Longer benefit period. 6, 9 or 12 months instead of ILOE's 3 months.
  • Total-salary basis. Premiums and payouts are calculated on gross monthly package (basic + housing + transport), not just basic.

Premiums — what to expect in 2026

Pricing for private job loss insurance UAE varies by:

  • Salary band — the higher the cover, the higher the premium.
  • Industry risk — tech, real estate and construction tend to price slightly higher than legal, finance or healthcare.
  • Benefit period — 12-month plans cost roughly twice 6-month plans.
  • Waiting period — most plans require 90–180 days of cover before a claim is valid. Shorter waiting periods cost more.

Indicative 2026 monthly premiums for a private redundancy top-up:

  • Junior professional, AED 15–20k total salary, 6-month benefit: AED 30 – 45/month.
  • Mid-senior, AED 25–40k total salary, 9-month benefit: AED 55 – 80/month.
  • Senior expat, AED 50–75k total salary, 12-month benefit: AED 90 – 180/month.

How a claim actually plays out

The chronology of a real-world UAE redundancy and claim, drawn from common insurer playbooks at Sukoon, Liva and Nextcare:

  1. Day 0: You receive a termination letter citing redundancy or restructuring. Your visa is on a 30-day grace period.
  2. Day 1–7: You file your ILOE claim through the iloe.ae portal or app. Documents required: cancelled work permit, last 6 payslips, Emirates ID, IBAN.
  3. Day 7–14: You file your private top-up claim. Most insurers now accept WhatsApp document uploads.
  4. Day 14–30: ILOE pays first — typically 60% of basic salary for the first month, into your bank.
  5. Day 30+: Private insurer tops up to your insured percentage of total salary. Payments continue monthly while you remain unemployed and resident in the UAE.
  6. End of benefit period: Cover ends. If you find a new job before then, payouts stop on the day you start.

Common exclusions you must read

Both ILOE and private job-loss policies share a similar exclusion list. Read it before you buy:

  • Voluntary resignation.
  • Termination for misconduct, disciplinary action or fraud.
  • Contract end (limited contracts that expire without renewal).
  • Job loss within the first 90–180 days of policy purchase (waiting period).
  • Termination during probation, in many private plans.
  • Pre-disclosed restructuring already announced before policy inception.
  • Self-employed individuals (a small number of insurers cover them with separate "freelancer" income-protection products).

Free-zone and non-MOHRE workers

If you work in a UAE free zone whose employer is not registered with MOHRE — DIFC, ADGM, JAFZA, DMCC and many others — your ILOE eligibility is more nuanced. The federal scheme has been progressively extended, but the cleanest answer in 2026 is:

  • Most free-zone employers are now linked through bilateral agreements with MOHRE. Confirm with HR.
  • If yours is not, your subscribe via the standalone ILOE Quick Pay portal as a private subscriber.
  • Private job-loss insurance UAE plans accept all free-zone workers without restriction.

How job loss insurance pairs with mortgages and school fees

The single most common reason UAE residents buy private redundancy cover is to keep mortgage EMIs and school fees paid through a forced career break. A typical AED 1.5 million mortgage at 4.5% over 25 years carries an EMI of around AED 8,300/month. A senior expat losing their job would receive — best case — AED 12,000 from ILOE Cat B (still capped). A 12-month private top-up bringing income to 70% of total salary turns that 3-month buffer into a year of breathing room. Pair it with mortgage insurance UAE (which protects against death or disability) and you have a complete debt-protection stack.

Choosing the right private plan — a 5-question checklist

  1. What % of total salary do I want replaced? 60% is standard, 80% is premium.
  2. How long do I want the benefit period? 6 months is the entry-level; 12 months is recommended above AED 25k salary.
  3. What is the waiting period? Pay slightly more for a 90-day plan over a 180-day plan if you suspect restructuring is near.
  4. Is it standalone or a rider on group cover? Standalone keeps cover when you change employer; rider does not.
  5. Who is the insurer's TPA? Nextcare, NAS and direct-billing partners materially speed up claim payout.

Is private job loss insurance UAE worth it?

For a household earning above AED 25,000 a month with mortgage, school fees or family commitments, the maths is almost always favourable. The cost — typically under 0.4% of monthly salary — buys 6–12 months of runway in a labour market that, while strong, has seen meaningful tech and consulting layoffs in 2024–26. For a junior professional under AED 15,000/month, ILOE alone is usually enough.
