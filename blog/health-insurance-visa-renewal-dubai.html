Health Insurance for Visa Renewal Dubai 2026 — What You Actually Need

By the InsuranceDubai.com editorial team · Published 1 May 2026 · 11 min read

Health insurance for visa renewal is one of the highest-intent search queries in the UAE insurance market — and one of the most poorly answered. The majority of people searching for it are 30 to 90 days away from a residence visa renewal deadline, and what they need is a precise, actionable answer: which plan qualifies, which insurers are accepted, what documents to submit, and how fast cover can be arranged. This guide provides exactly that.

⚠ Critical timing note: In Dubai, your health insurance must be in force before you can submit a visa renewal application through the ICA (Federal Authority for Identity and Citizenship) or AMER service centres. The system checks coverage in real time via the unified DHA-ICP insurance gateway. A policy applied for on the day of renewal submission may not register in the system for 24–48 hours, causing your application to fail. Apply for or confirm your insurance at least 3 working days before your visa renewal submission date.

Why health insurance is checked at visa renewal in Dubai

Health insurance became mandatory for Dubai residents under the Dubai Health Insurance Law (Law No. 11 of 2013). Since 2016, the DHA and ICP systems have been linked, meaning that when any Dubai resident — employee, dependent, or self-sponsored — applies to renew their UAE residence visa, the system automatically queries the DHA insurance registry to verify that a valid, DHA-compliant policy is in force for that person.

If no valid policy is found, the renewal application will be rejected outright. There is no grace period at the point of renewal, and there is no manual override process that the service centre staff can invoke. The verification is automated. This is why timing matters so much — getting the insurance sorted 3–5 working days before your visa renewal date gives the insurer enough time to register the policy in the DHA system, which is where ICP queries it.

What counts as a DHA-compliant health insurance plan?

Not all health insurance plans sold in the UAE qualify for Dubai visa purposes. The DHA maintains a list of approved insurers and a minimum benefit standard called the Essential Benefits Plan (EBP). Any plan that meets or exceeds the EBP criteria from a DHA-approved insurer will satisfy the visa requirement. The minimum EBP standard includes:

  • Emergency inpatient care up to AED 150,000 per year
  • Limited outpatient cover (GP and specialist visits) with a co-payment of up to 30%
  • Maternity complications (not normal delivery on basic plans)
  • Prescription drugs at in-network pharmacies
  • No permanent exclusion of pre-existing conditions (only a 6-month waiting period is permitted)
  • Cover valid across Dubai and the Northern Emirates

Plans that fall below the EBP standard — for example, travel insurance policies, standalone dental plans, or international plans that only cover treatment outside the UAE — do not qualify for Dubai visa purposes, regardless of how comprehensive they are for other uses. The DHA system specifically checks for registered UAE health policies, not general insurance.

Employees: what your employer's plan covers for visa renewal

If you are employed in Dubai and your employer provides group health insurance, that policy almost certainly qualifies for your visa renewal — provided it is from a DHA-approved insurer and meets the EBP minimum. Your employer is legally responsible for ensuring this is the case. You do not need to take any action other than confirming with your HR department that your policy is current and that your Emirates ID is correctly linked to it in the DHA system.

Where employee visa renewals are sometimes delayed is when:

  • The employer failed to renew the group policy on time (it lapsed between the renewal date and the next payment cycle)
  • The employee was incorrectly removed from the group policy during a payroll update
  • The group policy was changed to a new insurer at renewal and the new insurer's policy was not yet registered in the DHA system
  • The policy was registered under the wrong Emirates ID number

If your employer-provided cover is showing as inactive in the DHA system, do not wait. Contact your HR team immediately and ask them to confirm with the insurer that your Emirates ID is correctly linked and active in the DHA registry. Most insurers have a dedicated HR or group administration team that can resolve this within a few hours.

Dependents: insuring spouse and children for visa renewal

In Dubai, health insurance for dependents (spouse and children) is not mandated by law in the same way as for employees — but the practical reality is that a dependent's visa renewal will increasingly be checked for insurance coverage just as the employee's is. The ICP system flags uninsured dependents, and DHA has been progressively tightening enforcement since 2024.

For the most straightforward visa renewal experience, ensure every family member's Emirates ID is linked to a DHA-registered health insurance policy. For children who are being renewed for the first time (or for the first time since turning 18), make sure they have been added to the family policy with their own Emirates ID — not just their name on the policy document.

The most cost-efficient way to insure dependents for visa purposes is to add them to the sponsoring employee's group health insurance policy, if the employer allows it. Where the employer does not cover dependents, a family or individual policy from one of the DHA-approved insurers above will satisfy the requirement. Basic EBP-tier policies for a spouse or child typically cost AED 525–900 per person per year for young adults and children, making this one of the more affordable compliance requirements.

Self-sponsored residents and freelancers: buying cover for visa renewal

If you are self-sponsored in Dubai — this includes freelance visa holders, company owners, retirees on investor visas, and dependents who have converted to self-sponsored status — you are responsible for your own health insurance. The requirement is identical to that for employees: a DHA-compliant plan from an approved insurer, with your Emirates ID registered in the DHA system.

The underwriting process for self-sponsored individuals typically involves:

  • A health questionnaire (chronic conditions, previous surgeries, current medications)
  • For ages above 55 or declared conditions, a medical report from a DHA-approved facility may be required
  • Premiums that are 10–20% higher than equivalent group rates (because there is no group risk-pooling)

Lead time for self-sponsored applications is typically 3–7 working days from submission to DHA registration, longer if medical underwriting is required. Start the process at minimum 2 weeks before your visa renewal date if you are self-sponsored and buying cover for the first time.

What documents do you need?

For the visa renewal application itself, you do not need to submit a copy of your health insurance policy as a separate document — the DHA system verifies this automatically when ICP queries it. However, it is good practice to have the following ready in case of any discrepancy:

  • Your health insurance policy schedule (the document showing your name, Emirates ID, insurer name, policy number, and coverage dates)
  • Your insurance card (physical or digital)
  • Contact details for your insurer's customer service team (to resolve any registry discrepancies quickly)

If the ICA or AMER service centre tells you that your insurance is showing as inactive in the system, do not assume the system is wrong and do not wait. Call your insurer's direct line or your broker immediately and ask them to confirm registry status. DHA registry queries can be resolved quickly if escalated to the insurer's compliance team — most issues are resolved within 2–4 hours.

The AED 320 MOHRE Basic Plan and Dubai visa renewal

The federal AED 320 MOHRE Basic Health Insurance Plan — introduced in 2025 for employees in the Northern Emirates — is not a Dubai DHA-compliant EBP product. It is a MOHRE-registered federal product that satisfies visa renewal requirements for work permits in the Northern Emirates (Sharjah, Ajman, RAK, Fujairah, Umm Al Quwain) but is not registered in the DHA system and does not satisfy Dubai residence visa requirements.

This is one of the most common points of confusion for employees who move between emirates or whose employer is registered in one emirate but whose residence visa is in Dubai. If your work permit is a Dubai MOHRE permit, you need a DHA-registered plan. If your work permit is under the federal scheme (Northern Emirates), you need a policy registered with the relevant health authority for your residence visa emirate.

How much does the minimum qualifying plan cost?

A basic EBP-tier plan that satisfies Dubai visa renewal requirements costs approximately AED 525–900 per year for a healthy individual under 35, and AED 900–1,900 for individuals aged 35–50. For the policy to qualify, it must cover the DHA minimum benefit set described above and be issued by a DHA-approved insurer. Plans at this price point are available from Sukoon, Liva, NAS, GIG Gulf, and Dubai Insurance.

Many residents choose to buy a more comprehensive plan at visa renewal time — adding outpatient cover, dental, or maternity — since the incremental cost of upgrading to a mid-tier plan is modest (typically AED 400–1,200 more per year for a young adult) and the real-world benefit is substantially better. The EBP minimum is exactly that — a minimum. It is not a recommendation.
