Network Comparison — Where Can You Be Treated?

The network is the most important practical difference between GIG Gulf and Daman. It determines where you can walk in and get treated without paying upfront.

GIG Gulf Network

4,500+ direct-billing facilities across UAE

GIG Gulf (formerly AXA Gulf) operates the widest direct-billing network in the UAE by sheer facility count. In Dubai, this includes all major private hospitals — Medcare, American Hospital, Saudi German, Emirates Hospital, Mediclinic City, and most Aster and Aster Clinic branches. In Sharjah, Ras Al Khaimah, Ajman, Fujairah and Umm Al Quwain, GIG Gulf has the deepest penetration of any private insurer — critical for employees working in industrial or free-zone areas outside the main urban centres.

In Abu Dhabi, GIG Gulf covers most major private hospitals including Burjeel, Mediclinic, Danat Al Emarat and NMC, but does not have access to SEHA public facilities (Sheikh Khalifa Medical City, Al Ain Hospital, Tawam, etc.) — those are Daman-only.

Best for: Employees based in Dubai, the Northern Emirates, or any emirate other than Abu Dhabi. Also the best network for expats who want easy GP and specialist access across multiple cities.

Key GIG Gulf facilities in Dubai:

American Hospital Dubai · Medcare Hospitals · Saudi German Hospital · Emirates Hospital · Mediclinic City Hospital · Aster Hospital DIP · NMC Royal · King's College Hospital Dubai

Daman Network

3,200+ facilities; Abu Dhabi's dominant insurer

Daman (National Health Insurance Company) was established by the Abu Dhabi government in 2006 to administer the mandatory Thiqa scheme for UAE nationals and the Daman Enhanced plan for expat residents. Its dominance in Abu Dhabi is structural: it is the only insurer with direct-billing access to all SEHA-operated public hospitals and clinics, which cover the largest and most cost-efficient healthcare infrastructure in the emirate.

Outside Abu Dhabi, Daman has grown its Dubai and Northern Emirates presence considerably since 2022, but it remains second-tier by facility count in those markets. Its Dubai network covers Medcare, Aster, Saudi German, and NMC facilities but not the full range of Mediclinic or Emirates Hospital branches.

Best for: Employees and families based in Abu Dhabi or Al Ain. UAE nationals on the Thiqa scheme automatically receive Daman. Companies headquartered in Abu Dhabi with employees who regularly use public SEHA hospitals.

Key Daman facilities in Abu Dhabi:

Sheikh Khalifa Medical City · Tawam Hospital Al Ain · Al Ain Hospital · Mafraq Hospital · Burjeel Hospital · Mediclinic Abu Dhabi · Danat Al Emarat · NMC Royal Hospital Abu Dhabi

Claims — How Each Insurer Handles Your Claim

Claims handling is where most people discover the real quality of their insurer. Here is what to expect from each.

GIG Gulf Claims Process

For direct-billing (cashless) claims at network facilities, GIG Gulf operates a pre-authorisation system through its provider portal. Most pre-auths for outpatient visits are approved in real time at the reception desk — you show your insurance card, the clinic submits the pre-auth, and you pay only your co-payment. For inpatient admissions requiring more than AED 5,000 in cover, a pre-auth request is typically processed within 2–4 hours.

For reimbursement claims (when you visit a non-network provider or travel outside the UAE), the GIG Gulf mobile app and online portal allow you to upload invoices and supporting documents digitally. Standard reimbursement claims are settled within 7–10 working days. Disputes go to the claims team via a dedicated WhatsApp line, which most policyholders find considerably faster than a call centre.

Known weakness: Pre-authorisation for certain specialist referrals — particularly psychiatry, oncology second opinions, and some surgical procedures — can involve pushback from the medical review team, and appeals sometimes require a broker's intervention to escalate quickly.

Daman Claims Process

Daman operates what is broadly considered the most sophisticated digital claims platform of any UAE health insurer. The Daman Online portal (web and mobile) allows policyholders to check eligibility, view the provider network, file reimbursement claims, track status and download settlements — all without speaking to anyone. For SEHA public-hospital admissions in Abu Dhabi, the entire pre-authorisation and billing process is seamlessly integrated at the point of care; the patient simply shows their Emirates ID.

Reimbursement claims for outpatient visits to non-network providers are settled in 5–7 working days as a standard. Daman has invested heavily in AI-assisted claims triage since 2024, which has measurably reduced the rate of unnecessary delays on routine claims. For inpatient cases, Daman assigns a clinical case manager to admissions above AED 10,000.

Known weakness: Policyholders based in Dubai or the Northern Emirates with Daman cover sometimes experience delays when their treating physician is not pre-enrolled in the Daman provider portal — a more common issue outside Abu Dhabi than within it.

Employer & SME Group Plans — Which Works Better for Businesses?

Group health insurance for Dubai and UAE-based employers has different dynamics to individual plans. Here is how GIG Gulf and Daman stack up for companies of different sizes.

For Dubai-based SMEs (5–200 employees), GIG Gulf is consistently the more competitive insurer on both price and plan flexibility. Its SME product range — branded as GIG Gulf Corporate — allows employers to mix network tiers by employee grade, add dental, optical, mental health, and ILOE compliance integration in a single platform. For a 50-person Dubai SME insuring employees at the mid-tier, expect group rates 15–25% below individual retail pricing.

For Abu Dhabi-based employers with significant SEHA hospital usage, Daman's group plans are hard to beat. The Daman Corporate offering includes direct SEHA access, dedicated group account managers, and automatic integration with the Abu Dhabi DOH compliance system. Group plan premiums in Abu Dhabi are typically similar between GIG Gulf and Daman for non-SEHA access, but Daman wins clearly the moment SEHA cover is required.

For large corporates and multinationals (200+ employees), both insurers offer bespoke actuarially-priced group products where premium is driven by the group's actual claims experience. At this size, the network and plan design are more significant variables than the quoted rate. Most companies at this scale use an insurance broker rather than approaching insurers directly — the broker manages renewal negotiation, benchmarking and claims advocacy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about GIG Gulf vs Daman health insurance in the UAE.

Can I switch from GIG Gulf to Daman mid-year?

Switching mid-year is possible but carries risks. The new insurer will typically apply a new waiting period for maternity and pre-existing conditions. If you have already made claims in the current policy year, the new insurer will see that history at renewal underwriting. The best time to switch is at your renewal date, at least 60 days in advance, with an aggregator running parallel quotes from both so you can negotiate on a clean slate.

Does GIG Gulf or Daman cover pre-existing conditions?

Both insurers cover most pre-existing conditions after a standard 6-month waiting period on individual plans. Under the UAE's mandatory health insurance regulations, insurers cannot permanently exclude a pre-existing condition — they can only apply the 6-month waiting period. For group plans of 10 or more employees, the waiting period is typically waived entirely. Chronic conditions including diabetes, hypertension, and asthma are covered from month 7 onwards on individual plans at both insurers.

Is GIG Gulf the same as AXA Gulf?

Yes. GIG Gulf was previously known as AXA Gulf. The rebrand happened in 2022 following Gulf Insurance Group (GIG) completing its acquisition of AXA's operations in the MENA region. All legacy AXA Gulf policies, networks, and claims systems were migrated to the GIG Gulf brand. If you previously held an AXA Gulf policy, it is now a GIG Gulf policy — your cover, network and policy terms are unchanged by the rebrand.

Which insurer is better for a freelance or self-sponsored resident in Dubai?

For freelancers in Dubai, GIG Gulf is typically the better choice — broader Dubai hospital access, competitive individual premiums, and mid-tier plans that include maternity and mental health without requiring a premium product. Daman is the better choice for freelancers based in Abu Dhabi who want SEHA access. Both require individual underwriting for self-sponsored residents.
