How to Check Your ILOE Fine UAE — Step-by-Step Guide 2026

By the InsuranceDubai.com editorial team · Published 1 May 2026 · 8 min read

Searches for 'ILOE fine', 'ILOE insurance check' and 'ILOE fine check' have all gone Breakout on Google UAE in 2026 — and for good reason. Thousands of UAE employees who should have registered for the Involuntary Loss of Employment (ILOE) insurance scheme since its launch have either not registered at all or registered late, and are now discovering they have outstanding fines when they try to renew their visas or change jobs.

This guide tells you exactly how to check whether you have an ILOE fine, how much it is, and how to pay it — in under 2 minutes if you have your Emirates ID handy.

What is ILOE and why do fines exist?

The Involuntary Loss of Employment (ILOE) insurance scheme is a mandatory UAE government programme that provides financial support to employees who lose their jobs involuntarily. It launched in January 2023 under Cabinet Resolution No. 97 of 2022. All private sector employees on a UAE work permit, and most government employees, are legally required to subscribe.

The ILOE scheme is run by a consortium of insurers supervised by the Central Bank of the UAE. Premiums are collected monthly (AED 5/month for salaries under AED 16,000; AED 10/month for salaries over AED 16,000) and employees who lose their job involuntarily can claim 60% of their average salary for up to 3 months.

Why fines exist: Employees who did not register within 4 months of the scheme launch (or within 4 months of starting a new job) are subject to a late registration fine. The fine is AED 400 per month for every month they were eligible but unregistered. This can add up quickly if you joined the workforce in 2023 and have only just found out about the requirement.

How to check your ILOE fine — step by step

1

Go to the official ILOE portal

Visit iloe.ae in your browser. This is the only official portal for ILOE registration, payment, and fine checking. Do not use third-party sites claiming to offer ILOE services — the only authorised channels are iloe.ae, the MOHRE app, and official typing centres.

2

Log in with UAE Pass or Emirates ID

Click "Login" on the ILOE portal. You can log in using UAE Pass (the recommended option — it is faster and requires no separate password), or by entering your Emirates ID number and the OTP sent to your registered mobile number. If you have never accessed iloe.ae before, you will be prompted to verify your identity through UAE Pass first.

3

Navigate to "Fines and Violations"

Once logged in, you will see your ILOE dashboard showing your subscription status and payment history. Look for the "Fines and Violations" or "Penalties" section in the menu. If you have an outstanding fine, it will be displayed here with the amount and the period it covers.

4

Check the fine amount and period

The fine display will show you: the month(s) for which the fine applies, the AED 400/month rate, and the total outstanding amount. Verify that the dates are correct — if there is a discrepancy (for example, the system shows you as unregistered for a period when you were actually subscribed through a previous employer), you will need to file a dispute (see below).

5

Pay the fine online

Select "Pay Now" next to the outstanding fine. The portal accepts Visa, Mastercard, Mada (Saudi cards), and Apple Pay. Payment is processed immediately. You will receive a confirmation receipt by email and SMS. Save this receipt — you may need it as proof of payment during your next visa renewal.

6

Register for ILOE if you have not yet done so

After paying any outstanding fine, make sure your ILOE subscription is active going forward. From the dashboard, you can set up automatic monthly payment by credit card or link to your bank for direct debit. Monthly premiums are AED 5 (salary ≤AED 16,000) or AED 10 (salary >AED 16,000). This is the cheapest mandatory insurance in the UAE and automatic payment is strongly recommended to avoid future fines.

Alternative ways to check and pay your ILOE fine

Via the MOHRE smart app

Download the MOHRE app (available on iOS and Android). Go to "ILOE Services" within the app, log in with UAE Pass, and navigate to "My Fines". The MOHRE app also allows direct payment of ILOE fines by card.

By calling the ILOE helpline

Call 600 599 555 (ILOE customer service), available Sunday to Thursday 7:30am – 3:30pm. Have your Emirates ID number ready. The agent can confirm your registration status, outstanding fine amount, and guide you through the payment process by phone (though online payment is faster).

At a typing centre or ICA service centre

Any government-authorised typing centre in the UAE can check your ILOE status and process payment. This is the best option if you are having trouble accessing iloe.ae or do not have a smartphone. There is typically a small service fee (AED 10–30) charged by the typing centre on top of the fine amount.

What if I think my ILOE fine is wrong?

ILOE fine disputes do happen. Common reasons for incorrect fines include:

  • The system shows a fine for a period when you were subscribed through a previous employer
  • You changed jobs and your new employer registered you late without your knowledge
  • A technical error in Emirates ID linking between your old and new employer's payroll systems
  • You were on an unpaid leave period (maternity, study, illness) and the system did not pause the subscription correctly

To dispute an incorrect fine, log in to iloe.ae and use the "Raise a Complaint" function, or call 600 599 555. You will need to provide your Emirates ID, employment contract dates, and any evidence of previous ILOE registration (confirmation emails, payslips showing the deduction). Disputes are typically resolved within 5–10 working days.

Important: If your fine is under dispute, do not pay it before the dispute is resolved — payment is treated as acceptance of the fine amount and may make it harder to claim a refund if the dispute is found in your favour.

How to avoid ILOE fines in future

  • Set up automatic payment: Go to iloe.ae, link a credit card or bank account, and enable auto-renew. AED 5 or AED 10 per month will be deducted automatically — you will never miss a payment.
  • When changing jobs: Check your new employer has transferred your ILOE registration within 30 days of your new start date. Your employer is responsible for this, but it is worth confirming.
  • When going on leave: If taking unpaid leave of more than 30 days, ask your employer's HR team how they handle ILOE payments during leave. Some employers pay on your behalf; others pause the subscription (which may trigger a lapse fine if not handled correctly).
  • Keep your contact details updated: ILOE sends payment confirmations and renewal reminders by SMS and email to the contact details linked to your Emirates ID. If your phone number changes, update it in the ICA/UAEPASS system.

Frequently asked questions

Can my employer pay my ILOE fine on my behalf?

Technically yes — an employer can make the payment through their company account on the ILOE portal. However, the fine is the employee's legal obligation. Some employers do pay ILOE fines as a goodwill gesture when they realise their HR team failed to register a new employee on time, but there is no legal requirement for them to do so. If your employer's delay caused the fine, it is worth raising this with HR before paying personally.

Will an unpaid ILOE fine block my visa renewal?

Yes. Outstanding ILOE fines are linked to your Emirates ID in the government system and can block visa processing. This is becoming more consistently enforced in 2025–2026. If you have an unresolved ILOE fine and are approaching a visa renewal date, pay or dispute the fine well in advance — do not leave it until the renewal appointment.

I left the UAE — do I still owe the ILOE fine?

If you have cancelled your UAE visa and left the country, the ILOE fine technically still exists on the system linked to your Emirates ID. In practice, it is only enforced when you re-enter the UAE or apply for a new residency. If you plan to return to the UAE for work in future, it is cleaner to resolve the fine before or during your departure process — it can be paid online from outside the UAE through iloe.ae.

What is the ILOE insurance itself — separate from the fine?

ILOE is job loss insurance, not health insurance. If you are made redundant involuntarily, you can claim 60% of your average salary (capped at AED 20,000/month) for up to 3 months while you look for new work. Claims are made through iloe.ae within 30 days of losing your job. The monthly premium (AED 5 or AED 10) pays for this benefit.
