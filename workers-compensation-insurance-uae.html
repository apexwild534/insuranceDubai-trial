Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about this insurance type in Dubai and UAE.

Is workers compensation insurance mandatory in UAE?

Workers compensation is required under the UAE Workmen Compensation Law (Federal Law No. 8 of 1980 as amended) for all private sector employers. The law mandates specific compensation levels for injury, death and disability.

What compensation is owed for a work injury in UAE?

Under UAE law, the minimum compensation for total permanent disability is AED 18,000 per year for 3 years. Death compensation is typically AED 18,000 per year × 3 years plus funeral costs and repatriation.

Does workers compensation cover domestic workers in UAE?

Yes. Domestic workers in the UAE, including maids, drivers and nannies, are covered under the 2017 Domestic Workers Law and are entitled to compensation for work-related injuries.

How much does workers compensation insurance cost in UAE?

Premiums are based on your payroll, number of employees and industry sector. Typical rates range from 0.2–1.5% of annual payroll. Manual and hazardous industries attract higher rates.
