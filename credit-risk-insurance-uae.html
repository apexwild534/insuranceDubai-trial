What is Credit Risk Insurance?

Credit risk insurance — also known as trade credit insurance — protects UAE businesses against non-payment of trade debts.

In the UAE's dynamic trading environment, extending credit to buyers always carries risk. When a buyer defaults, your business faces cash flow crises, bad debt write-offs, and potentially existential financial pressure. Credit risk insurance eliminates this uncertainty.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about this insurance type in Dubai and UAE.

What is credit risk insurance in UAE?

Credit risk insurance protects UAE businesses against non-payment by buyers due to insolvency, protracted default, or political events. It typically covers 80–90% of the invoice value.

What is the difference between credit risk and trade credit insurance?

They are the same product — 'trade credit insurance' and 'credit risk insurance' are interchangeable terms used by different insurers and brokers.

How much does credit risk insurance cost in UAE?

Premiums are typically 0.1–0.5% of insured turnover depending on buyer quality, sector, geography and credit terms. Contact us for a tailored UAE quotation.

Does credit risk insurance cover export sales?

Yes. Export credit insurance covers overseas buyers and can include political risk cover for challenging jurisdictions, making it ideal for UAE exporters.
