Employer Insurance Obligations in Dubai & UAE

As an employer in the UAE, you have specific legal obligations to insure your employees. Non-compliance carries significant fines. Here is everything you need to know.

What Employers Must Provide by Law

🏥 Health Insurance (Dubai Employers)

Under the Dubai Health Insurance Law (Law No. 11 of 2013), all employers in Dubai must provide health insurance for every employee. For employees earning under AED 4,000/month, the Essential Benefits Plan (EBP) with a premium of approximately AED 650–750/year is the minimum compliant option. For higher earners, a broader network plan is typically required. Failure to comply carries fines of up to AED 500 per uninsured employee per month.

🩺 Federal Basic Health Plan (Northern Emirates)

From 1 January 2025, MOHRE mandates the AED 320/year Basic Health Plan for all private sector employees in Abu Dhabi, Sharjah, Ajman, Umm Al Quwain, Ras Al Khaimah and Fujairah who are not otherwise insured. Employers must enrol employees before visa renewal or face rejection of the visa application entirely.

📜 ILOE (Involuntary Loss of Employment Insurance)

All private sector employees in the UAE (and most free zone employees) must be enrolled in the MOHRE ILOE scheme. Premiums are paid by the employee (from AED 5–10/month depending on salary) but it is the employer's responsibility to ensure enrolment has been completed. MOHRE imposes penalties on employees — and reputational risk falls on the employer if visa processing is blocked.

⚠️ Workers' Compensation Insurance

UAE Labour Law requires employers to compensate employees for work-related injuries or death. While not always mandated as a standalone policy, employers in construction, manufacturing, and industrial sectors are strongly advised — and in many cases contractually required — to carry workers' compensation and employers' liability cover. Claims without insurance cover can expose directors to personal liability.

Common Employer Questions

What happens if I do not insure my employees in Dubai?

MOHRE and DHA conduct random inspections and cross-check visa renewal applications against insurance records. Uninsured employees result in fines of AED 500 per employee per month, and visa renewals for the affected employees can be blocked until cover is arranged and confirmed. Repeated violations can escalate to trading licence suspension.

Does the AED 320 MOHRE plan satisfy Dubai health insurance law?

No. The AED 320 MOHRE Basic Health Plan satisfies the federal mandate for the Northern Emirates only. Dubai employers must provide the Dubai Health Authority Essential Benefits Plan (EBP) as the minimum — which has a broader network and higher premium. If your business is based in Dubai but employs staff who reside outside Dubai, you need to comply with both regimes.

Can I deduct health insurance premiums from employee salaries?

Under Dubai law, the employer is required to bear the cost of the minimum mandated health insurance plan. You cannot legally deduct the EBP premium from an employee's salary. However, if the employer provides a plan above the minimum — for example an enhanced plan with dental or optical — the employee can be asked to contribute toward the upgrade portion only.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about insurance in Dubai and the UAE.

Is health insurance mandatory in Dubai?

Yes. Under the Dubai Health Insurance Law, all employers must provide health insurance for their employees. Self-employed residents and dependents must also have valid health coverage to renew their residence visas.

How much does life insurance cost in Dubai?

Term life insurance in Dubai starts from approximately AED 40–80 per month for a healthy non-smoker aged 30–40. Premiums vary based on age, health, coverage amount and policy term.

Can expats get life insurance in the UAE?

Absolutely. Most UAE insurers offer life insurance to expatriates living and working in Dubai and the wider UAE, with policies tailored for expat needs including repatriation cover.
